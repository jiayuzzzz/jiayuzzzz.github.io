Changes in the real estate rental market can be related to many factors, such as the overall economic and social environment. Among them, the number of population represents the demand situation in the real estate market. Generally, a higher population represents a higher demand, and according to the supply and demand relationship, an increase in demand will drive an increase in rental prices, which will lead to an increase in market supply. Similarly, we assume that the number of people employed is related to the economic level of people, and that more people find jobs in The Hague may mean a higher demand for housing.

My hypothesis is the residential properties avaliable for rent will be more, with less population, less employments and less average home occupation.

Variables are showed:
1). Average House Occupation
2). Employement 2018 - Neighbourhoods
3). Population 2018 - Neighbourhoods (this variable is represented by number of households)
4). Housing available for rent 2018 - Neighbourhoods (this variable is used to represent vacancies)

There is evidence that the real estate vacancy is somewhat related to population, and in general, a large population and a relatively tight housing demand market, both for housing sales and rentals, will cause decrease of the amount of remaining houses available for rent; however, this is not absolute, and also depends on the housing price market and willingness to rent, as more demand will cause a corresponding rise in supply in a short period of time, thus reaching a new market equilibrium point.

The same applies to employment rates; in general, the greater the probability that people will find jobs and the higher the economic level, the higher the average occupancy rate of dwellings, the lower vacancies. However, this does not necessarily fit perfectly. A growing metropolis with more jobs will attract migrant workers, who usually do not purchase homes, and in cities with low vacancy rates, rental prices are bound to rise due to excessive demand, and thus some people cannot afford the high cost of renting a home.For the average housing occupancy, there is generally a negative correlation with the housing avaliable for rent.

This model shows that the number of houses available for rent has a positive relationship with the number of shopping destinations in the neighborhood and the number of people arriving in The Hague, and a negative relationship with ln (the average house occupancy rate) and the crime rate. The most significant relationship is with ln (average house occupancy), as its coefficient is -4.9673, followed by the number of shopping outlets, with a coefficient of 0.2262.

This result can be explained：

First, people are more inclined to rent houses in areas with convenient living, so there is a higher demand for houses with supermarkets nearby, even if they are also slightly more expensive, which will stimulate supply in a short period of time, and homeowners will be more confident about the performance of houses with better surrounding facilities in the rental market as well.

Total arrivals can be considered as an explanation for migrant workers, the more people find work in The Hague, the stronger the demand for rental housing, and likewise, the increased demand will stimulate the market price, and households will be more inclined to rent their homes.

A plausible explanation for the crime rate is that houses in neighborhoods with higher crime rates are inherently harder to sell in the buying and selling market, while people do not prioritize housing in neighborhoods with higher crime rates, and thus the number of houses available for rent in neighborhoods with higher crime rates decreases. The relationship between LN(average home occupancy) and rentable homes is roughly the same as our hypothesis, where a higher average home occupancy means that the more residents living in a property, the fewer opportunities there are for renting.

Discussion¶

By comparing the images of y test value and the 23 predicted values of y(Number of houses available for renting) based on the training model, we can see that the model fit is not particularly accurate. Starting with the 3rd value, the basic trend matches up to the 15th value. The reason for such a result is most likely due to the R-squared being too small, which could be due to：

  • the sample size of the model is too small, resulting in a biased fit.
  • The final model we chose has an R-square = 0.500, which means that only half of the variables are fully explained by our model.
    Therefore, it can be assumed that there are many other possible correlates, such as the number of schools in the neighborhood, the state of public transportation, the number of sports facilities and cultural facilities, and other infrastructures that can fully highlight the convenience of living. When the living conditions are more complete, based on our fitted model, we can initially speculate that the rise in demand causes an increase in supply and therefore a positive correlation may exist.

In addition to this, demographic characteristics may also have a linear relationship with the dependent variable, where the more single people there are, then there will be more rentable space for a property relative to a large family. Overall, a large part of the reason the model is not sufficiently accurate is that the independent variables that significantly affect the dependent variable are not included. So the model can be improved by adding more explanatory variables and then performing regression analysis in the future. And if the model is sufficiently well developed (rather than a simple linear regression), this type of analysis can provide real estate agents with a rough estimate of pricing levels by forecasting the market for available rental housing.
